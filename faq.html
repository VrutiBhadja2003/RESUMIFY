CV (Curriculum Vitae)


Information, Frequently Asked Questions, and Tips on Your CV.

What is a CV?

- CV 'Curriculum Vitae', which mean 'life course'. A professional CV provides a summary and a good overview of someone's professional life

- CV can include academic profile, qualifications, certifications, work experience, skills, and important qualities. By CV, your potential employer will be able to get a good picture of your skills, work experience, and knowledge quickly, to assess whether or not you fit the job, and therefore whether to offer you a job interview.

RESUME VS CV

- A resume is typically a one or two-page document that summarizes your work experience, education, skills, and achievements. It is generally used in the United States and Canada, and is most commonly used when applying for non-academic positions..

- A CV, on the other hand, is a more comprehensive document that can be several pages long. It includes a detailed summary of your academic and professional background, including publications, research projects, teaching experience, and other achievements. It is typically used in the UK, Europe, and other countries outside of North America, and is most commonly used when applying for academic or research positions.

What Does A Good Resume Includes?

- Tailor your resume to the job: Customize your resume for each job application. Highlight your most relevant skills and experience that match the job requirements.
-Use a clear and easy-to-read format: Use a simple and consistent font, bullet points, and headings to make your resume easy to read. Avoid using fancy fonts or graphics.
-Start with a strong objective or summary statement: A well-written objective or summary statement can grab the employer's attention and highlight your key strengths and qualifications.
-Use keywords: Many employers use applicant tracking systems (ATS) to scan resumes for specific keywords. Use keywords from the job description to ensure your resume gets past the ATS.
-Keep it concise: Keep your resume to one or two pages. Be selective about what you include, and prioritize the most important information.
-Be honest: Don't exaggerate your skills or experience on your resume. Employers will verify your information, and dishonesty can damage your reputation.
-Include relevant education and certifications: List any relevant degrees, courses, or certifications that you have earned.
-Update your resume regularly: Update your resume regularly to reflect any new skills, experience, or achievements.

Tips For Giving Outstanding Interview

- Sound Confident: Answer all the questions after evaluating it with your complete knowledge, IQ and Logic keeping presence of mind.
- Dress appropriately: Dress professionally and appropriately for the interview. This shows that you take the opportunity seriously and are respectful of the interviewer's time.
- Introduction: Introduce youself in very unique and Creative way, use phrases or rhyming words if you can, tell them about things you excel in and try to communicate well.
- Show enthusiasm: Show enthusiasm for the job and the company throughout the interview. Smile, maintain eye contact, and express your interest in the position and the company.
- Listen carefully: Listen carefully to the interviewer's questions and respond thoughtfully. If you're not sure about a question, ask for clarification before answering.
- Ask thoughtful questions: Prepare a few thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer at the end of the interview. This shows that you are genuinely interested in the company and the job.
